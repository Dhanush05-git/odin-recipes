Egg Noodles

Description

Egg noodles are a classic street-food favorite, consisting of long, springy noodles tossed with scrambled eggs and crisp vegetables. The dish is known for its savory, salty, and slightly tangy profile.

The texture is a delightful mix of soft, slippery noodles and firm, protein-rich egg bits, all stir-fried at high heat to achieve that characteristic "wok-hei" or smoky aroma.

Ingredients

  • Boiled Noodles
  • Onions
  • Chilli
  • Oil
  • Eggs
  • Veggies (Cabbage/Carrots/Capsicum)
  • Salt
  • Masala powder (or Noodle Masala)

Steps

  1. Scramble Eggs: Heat oil in a wok or large pan. Whisk the eggs with a pinch of salt, pour them in, and scramble until fluffy. Remove and set aside.
  2. Flash-Fry Aromatics: Add more oil to the pan and sauté onions and green chillies on high heat until the onions are just soft.
  3. Stir-fry Veggies: Toss in your shredded veggies. Stir-fry quickly on high heat so they remain crunchy and colorful.
  4. Seasoning: Add the masala powder and salt. Mix well with the vegetables to create a flavorful base.
  5. Toss Noodles: Add the boiled noodles to the pan. Using tongs or two forks, toss them vigorously with the veggies and spices to ensure an even coating.
  6. Final Combine: Fold the scrambled eggs back into the noodles. Give it a final high-heat stir-fry for 1-2 minutes and serve hot.
